When I'm Usually Brought In

Most early-stage teams hit a point where growth starts creating friction instead of momentum. That's when I'm typically called in:

  • Operations are fragmented, manual, and reactive
  • Founders are overloaded and stuck in day-to-day execution
  • Teams are scaling faster than processes
  • Decisions are made with limited visibility or data
  • Execution depends too heavily on a few people

At this stage, hiring more people doesn't solve the problem. The company needs an operating system.
